How It Works

PDFNest renders each visible page and rebuilds a new PDF from those rendered pages. The result is a flattened copy.

  1. Select one PDF file.
  2. Choose standard, better, or high render quality.
  3. Flatten and download the new PDF.

Common Use Cases

Flatten signed PDFs, annotated documents, filled forms, proof files, or documents where you want the visible appearance preserved.

Privacy

Flattening happens locally in your browser. The selected PDF is not uploaded to a server.

Limitations

The flattened output is image-based. File size can increase, text may not be selectable, and very large PDFs can take longer on mobile devices.

Tips for Best Results

  • Flatten after signing, annotating, or filling visible text onto a PDF.
  • Keep the original PDF if you may need editable text or form fields later.
  • Use flattening when you want a simpler visible-page copy for sharing.

Before You Download

Open the flattened output and confirm visible signatures, notes, and form appearances are included.

Privacy and Ads

The tool runs in your browser and keeps document processing separate from advertising areas. PDFNest does not send selected document contents to an ad server, and ad containers should stay away from upload, action, and download controls.

FAQ

Why would I flatten a PDF?

Flattening makes the visible result less editable and can help preserve signatures, annotations, and form appearances.

Can I choose quality?

Yes. Higher quality uses more memory and may create a larger PDF.
