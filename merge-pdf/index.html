How It Works

Open the merge tool, select two or more PDF files, then review the editable page list before creating the final document. You can reorder files, preview pages, rotate pages, remove pages you do not need, and download one merged PDF.

  1. Select PDF files in the order you want them combined.
  2. Use previews and drag sorting to adjust pages.
  3. Click Merge PDFs and download the finished file.

Common Use Cases

Merge PDF is useful for combining invoices, scanned pages, contracts, school documents, receipts, reports, and application paperwork into one easier-to-share file.

Privacy

Selected PDFs are read and combined locally in your browser. PDFNest does not upload the documents to a server, and temporary download links are cleared when the browser session ends.

Limitations

Very large files can be slower depending on your device memory. Password-protected PDFs may preview with a password, but editing and final merging require unlocked PDFs.

Tips for Best Results

  • Put files in the final reading order before merging.
  • Preview pages after reordering so cover pages, signatures, and attachments stay where expected.
  • Remove blank scans or duplicate pages before creating the final PDF.

Before You Download

Open the merged preview list and confirm the page order, page count, and rotated pages before downloading the final file.

Privacy and Ads

The tool runs in your browser and keeps document processing separate from advertising areas. PDFNest does not send selected document contents to an ad server, and ad containers should stay away from upload, action, and download controls.

FAQ

Does the Merge PDF tool upload my files?

No. PDFNest merges selected PDF files locally in your browser and does not upload documents to a server.

Can I change page order before merging?

Yes. You can preview, reorder, rotate, or remove pages before creating the merged PDF.
