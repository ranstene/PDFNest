How It Works

Open the Word tool, select DOCX files, choose basic page settings, and convert the document text into a PDF. The browser reads the DOCX package and creates a new downloadable PDF.

  1. Select one or more DOCX files.
  2. Choose page size, orientation, margins, and font size.
  3. Convert and download the PDF output.

Common Use Cases

Convert simple letters, notes, outlines, drafts, homework, basic tables, meeting notes, and plain document text into PDF format.

Privacy

The DOCX file is read in your browser. The tool creates a new PDF download without uploading your document to a server.

Limitations

This browser-only converter is best for simple DOCX files. Complex page layouts, comments, tracked changes, custom fonts, headers, footers, charts, and advanced formatting may not match Word exactly.

Tips for Best Results

  • Use DOCX files with simple text layouts for the most predictable browser-only conversion.
  • Check headings, lists, and spacing after conversion.
  • Use a desktop office app for complex documents with advanced formatting.

Before You Download

Open the generated PDF and compare important text, headings, and page breaks before sharing.

Privacy and Ads

The tool runs in your browser and keeps document processing separate from advertising areas. PDFNest does not send selected document contents to an ad server, and ad containers should stay away from upload, action, and download controls.

FAQ

Does the Word to PDF tool upload my document?

No. PDFNest converts DOCX files locally in your browser and does not upload documents to a server.

Does Word to PDF preserve exact DOCX layout?

The browser-only converter is best for text, headings, lists, and basic tables. Complex Word layouts may not match exactly.
