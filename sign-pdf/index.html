How It Works

Open the sign tool, select a PDF, then draw a signature, type a signature, or use the full-view signer to place marks on a page preview. The tool creates a new signed PDF copy for download.

  1. Select one PDF file.
  2. Draw or type your signature and choose the page position.
  3. Download the signed PDF copy.

Common Use Cases

Add a simple signature mark to forms, approvals, drafts, school documents, client PDFs, internal paperwork, and everyday files before sharing.

Privacy

Your browser reads the selected PDF, places your signature into a new PDF copy, and creates a temporary download link. Refreshing or closing the page clears selected files and generated links.

Limitations

This is a visual signing tool, not a certificate-based digital signature system. It does not verify identity, add cryptographic signatures, or edit encrypted PDFs without an unlocked copy.

Tips for Best Results

  • Use full-view signing when placement matters on forms or approval pages.
  • Keep the signature size readable but not so large that it covers document text.
  • Use typed signatures for quick approvals and drawn signatures when a handwritten look is needed.

Before You Download

Confirm the signature page, position, color, and size before downloading the signed PDF.

Privacy and Ads

The tool runs in your browser and keeps document processing separate from advertising areas. PDFNest does not send selected document contents to an ad server, and ad containers should stay away from upload, action, and download controls.

FAQ

Does the Sign PDF tool upload my file?

No. PDFNest signs PDFs locally in your browser and does not upload your document or signature to a server.

Can I choose where the signature goes?

Yes. You can draw directly on a full-page preview or place a drawn or typed signature at a chosen position.
