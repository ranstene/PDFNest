How It Works

Open the spreadsheet tool, select XLSX or CSV files, then choose page size, orientation, margins, and table display options. PDFNest reads worksheet data and creates a readable PDF table.

  1. Select XLSX or CSV spreadsheet files.
  2. Choose page layout and table options.
  3. Convert and download the PDF output.

Common Use Cases

Convert simple budgets, contact lists, inventory tables, schedules, logs, reports, CSV exports, and spreadsheet records into PDFs.

Privacy

Spreadsheet files are read locally in your browser. PDFNest does not upload XLSX or CSV files to a server.

Limitations

The converter focuses on worksheet data and readable tables. It does not preserve every chart, formula style, macro, merged-cell layout, image, or complex Excel design.

Tips for Best Results

  • Use clean tables with clear headers for the easiest PDF output.
  • Choose landscape orientation for wide spreadsheets.
  • Limit very large sheets or export one important table at a time.

Before You Download

Review column fit, row visibility, and page orientation before downloading the spreadsheet PDF.

Privacy and Ads

The tool runs in your browser and keeps document processing separate from advertising areas. PDFNest does not send selected document contents to an ad server, and ad containers should stay away from upload, action, and download controls.

FAQ

Does the Excel to PDF tool upload my spreadsheet?

No. PDFNest converts spreadsheet data locally in your browser and does not upload XLSX or CSV files to a server.

Does Excel to PDF preserve every spreadsheet layout?

The converter is best for readable worksheet tables. It does not preserve every chart, formula style, or complex Excel layout.
